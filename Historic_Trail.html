National Historic Trail

A National Historic Trail is a protected area in the United States containing historic trails and surrounding areas. They are part of the National Trails System and are designated to protect the remains of significant overland or water routes to reflect the history of the nation.

There are 19 National Historic Trails in the US.
Name Location Length Description
Ala Kahakai National Historic Trail Hawaii 175 miles This trail was established to access the traditional Ancient Hawaiian culture along with the natural geology of the island
California Trail From the Missouri River to California 3000 miles An emigrant trail across the western half of the North American continent
Captain John Smith Chesapeake National Historic Trail Virginia 3000 miles A series of water routes along the Chesapeake Bay that Captain John Smith used to chart the land and waterways of the Chesapeake
El Camino Real de los Tejas National Historic Trail Louisiana to Texas 2580 miles Covers the US section of the El Camino Real de Los Tejas from 17th century Spanish colonial era in Spanish Texas
El Camino Real de Tierra Adentro National Historic Trail Texas and New Mexico 404 miles The US section of the El Camino Real de Tierra Adentro, a 1600 mile long trade route between Mexico City and New Mexico
Iditarod Trail Alaska 2350 miles The historic and contemporary trail system in the US state of Alaska
Juan Bautista de Anza National Historic Trail Arizona and California 1210 miles This trail commemorates the land route that Spanish commander Juan Bautista de Anza took from Colonial Mexico through to Las Californias Province
Lewis and Clark National Historic Trail Illinois to Oregon 3700 miles This trail commmemorates the Lewis and Clark Expedition of 1804 to 1806
Mormon Pioneer National Historic Trail Illinois to Utah 1300 miles This trail commemorates the route that members of The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints traveled from 1846 to 1868
Nez Perce National Historic Trail Oregon to Montana 1170 miles This trail follows the route taked by a large band of the Nez Perce Indian tribe during their attempt to flee from the US Cavalry and get to Canada
Old Spanish National Historic Trail New Mexico to California 700 miles A historical trade route that connected the northen New Mexican settlements with those of California
Oregon National Historic Trail Illinois to Oregon 2170 miles This trail follows the historic east-west large-wheeled wagon route and emigrant trail that connected the Missouri River to valleys in Oregon
Overmountain Victory National Historic Trail Virginia to Tennessee 275 miles This trail recognizes the Revolutionary War Overmountain Men who crossed the Great Smoky Mountains
Pony Express National Historic Trail Missouri to California 1966 miles This trail perserves the original Pony Express trail and to continue the memory and importance of the Pony Express
Santa Fe National Historic Trail Missouri to New Mexico 1203 miles 19th century transportation route through central North America. Was a vital highway until the introduction of the railroad
Selma to Montgomery National Historic Trail Alabama 54 miles Three protect marches were held along this 54-mile highway. The marches were organized by activists in defiance of segregationist repression
Star-Spangled Banner National Historic Trail Virginia 290 miles This trail commemorates the Chesapeake Campaign of the War of 1812 and is named after the national anthem
Trail of Tears National Historic Trail Hawaii 2200 miles This trail follows the forced removals of Native American nations from their ancestral homelands in the Southeastern United States to an area west of the Mississippi River
Washington-Rochambeau Revolutionary Route National Historic Trail Rhode Island to Virginia 680 miles This trail follows the long series of roads used by the Continental Army under Washington and the Expédition Particulière under Rochambeau
